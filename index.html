Rule 1 — the ending gives the clue

Non-metals ending in -ide: charge −1 (Cl⁻, Br⁻, OH⁻) or −2 (O²⁻, S²⁻).
Non-metals ending in -ate: always a group ion — SO₄²⁻, CO₃²⁻ (−2) or NO₃⁻ (−1).

Rule 2 — group number = charge

Group 1 → always +1. Group 2 → always +2. Aluminium (Group 3) → +3.
Na is Group 1 → Na⁺. Ca is Group 2 → Ca²⁺. Al is Group 3 → Al³⁺.

Rule 3 — link symbol to real life

Na = table salt. Ca = your bones. Mg = white lab flame. Cu = copper pipes. Al = foil. Fe = iron/steel (Ferrum). K = banana (potassium).

Rule 4 — same charge → one of each

If both ions have the same number (e.g. Cu²⁺ and O²⁻) you only need ONE of each → CuO, not Cu₂O₂.

Rule 5 — OH⁻ comes from water

Water = H₂O. When a metal reacts with water, the water splits and gives OH (hydroxide) to the product. "OH no, it's negative!" — always −1, always from water reactions.
